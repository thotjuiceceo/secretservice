A year later, and I have still never met anyone like LeeAnn. A woman with a feral ferocity for nurturing the song-birds and land rodents that your average corn-fed Missourian shoots off their back patio. If you're my mother's neighbors in Hilsboro, you might even go as far as to skin and eat them (I'm not worried about them reading this: they don't have wifi). Anyways: That unconditional love that LeeAnn shows all critters: I couldn't help but have a deep respect for that.

Another year, another baby season. About the only thing that had changed was that this go-around, LeeAnn was operating on a full six hours of sleep (over the last five days). I consider this a vast improvement. This year, we tackled the possum enclosure. The mother and her babies (enough to rival that of a Catholic household) had recently been moved out and reintroduced to nature. They were kind enough to leave behind about a month's worth of urine-soaked litter. As we overturned dried litter chips, we were hit with ammonia fumes strong enough to warrant a raid by Homeland Security (heaven forbid a stack of federal agents ever wind up on LeeAnn's porch). Cleaning up after the possums made hosing down the crates look glamorous.

![](/images/2025/glamour.jpg "Glamour")

Also on this year's To-Do list was cleaning the shelter that is used for Wintering the animals undergoing rehabilitation. This shelter was once the temporary home of a baby squirrel named Ricky. I found Ricky in the late Fall of 2023 behind a dumpster (not an uncommon place to find a Ricky). Or should I say, the German Shepherd I was pet-sitting found him. At first, I thought the dog (unfortunately named Elon) had found one of his toys, as he had his head shoved between my trash bins and I heard a squeaking noise. After disovering Ricky, I was quite relieved to find that the source of the squeaking sound was not Elon using the baby squirrel as a chew toy. I contacted LeeAnn, as I found it odd that this baby squirrel was all alone and not running off. LeeAnn viewed some photos I took of Ricky, and verified that he was starving and would most certainly die of exposure overnight (this was the first freeze of the season). After a Summer-long war of attrition  against the neighborhood squirrels over the contents of my garden, I was reluctant to help one of their kind. Nevertheless, I put Ricky in a shoe box with a warm blanket (wash rag) and drove him up to Wildlife Mediation. It was just a baby, after all. LeeAnn thanked me and named the Squirrel Ricky after me. This is the story of how I became acquainted with LeeAnn.

Fast-forward back to 2025. LeeAnn takes us out back and mentions that just about all of the squirrels in the area are squirrels that she has rehabbed (there are a LOT of squirrels). I ask if she can tell them apart. She answers my question by pointing out Ricky. I ask her how she can tell its him. She chuckles and tells me to take a look for myself. I get closer and it is immediately apparant: Ricky is hung like a horse.

Anyways, the shelter had been breached by rats over the Winter, and was littered with rat droppings. Wanting to minimize the risk of hantavirus exposure, I tell my friends that I'm going to knock this one out on my own (I reassure them that its way worse than whatever they are doing, which in hindsight was a very suspicious thing to say). I filled a family-sized trash bin full of nasties and fed it to the ever-hungry sinkhole. 

The approach of rain had us pivot to cleaning out the garage full of donations and storage supplies. It is cold. We are drenched and covered in wild animal nastiness. The support staff are chain-smoking cigarettes. Morale is low. We finally finish. LeeAnn smokes another cigarette, then enters her house. She returns with two baby raccoons eager to thank us for our hard work. Its cold out, and the racoons keep trying to crawl into our shirts to stay warm. 

![](/images/2025/raccooncold.jpg "United at last")

Meeting the animals was unforgettable and instantly made everything worth it.

![](/images/2025/worthit.jpg "Worth It.")

Oh yeah, the carpentry team missed their chance to meet baby raccoons last year (the meet & greet was a welcome surprise). After a full year of FOMO, they finally got their chance.

Next year we hope to land a Marlboro Reds sponsorship.
